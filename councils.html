First Council of Nicaea (325)

Defined the Nicene Creed, condemned Arianism, and affirmed the divinity of Jesus Christ.

First Council of Constantinople (381)

Completed the Nicene Creed and affirmed the divinity of the Holy Spirit against heresies.

Council of Ephesus (431)

Declared the Blessed Virgin Mary as Theotokos (Mother of God) and condemned Nestorianism.

Council of Chalcedon (451)

Defined that Christ is one Person with two natures, fully God and fully man.

Second Council of Constantinople (553)

Condemned the Three Chapters and defended the teachings of the Council of Chalcedon.

Third Council of Constantinople (680–681)

Condemned Monothelitism and affirmed that Christ possesses both a divine and a human will.

Second Council of Nicaea (787)

Restored the veneration of holy images and condemned the Iconoclast heresy.

Fourth Council of Constantinople (869–870)

Condemned Photius and reaffirmed papal authority and Church unity.

First Lateran Council (1123)

Confirmed the Concordat of Worms and enacted reforms concerning clergy and Church discipline.

Second Lateran Council (1139)

Strengthened clerical discipline and declared clerical marriages invalid.

Third Lateran Council (1179)

Required a two-thirds majority for papal elections and introduced important disciplinary reforms.

Fourth Lateran Council (1215)

Defined the doctrine of Transubstantiation and required annual confession and Holy Communion.

First Council of Lyon (1245)

Deposed Emperor Frederick II and addressed reforms and preparations for a crusade.

Second Council of Lyon (1274)

Sought reunion with the Eastern Church and established norms for papal conclaves.

Council of Vienne (1311–1312)

Suppressed the Knights Templar and addressed reforms within the Church.

Council of Constance (1414–1418)

Ended the Western Schism by electing Pope Martin V and condemned the teachings of Jan Hus.

Council of Basel–Ferrara–Florence (1431–1445)

Worked toward reunion with the Eastern Churches and discussed Church reform and doctrine.

Fifth Lateran Council (1512–1517)

Promoted Church reform and reaffirmed Catholic teachings shortly before the Protestant Reformation.

Council of Trent (1545–1563)

Clarified Catholic doctrine, reformed Church practices, and responded to the Protestant Reformation.

First Vatican Council (1869–1870)

Defined the doctrine of Papal Infallibility and reaffirmed the authority of the Roman Pontiff.
